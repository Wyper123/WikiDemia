The Sports Track aims to provide you with an understanding of the fundamental principles and techniques of physical education and recreation. It will also provide you with the opportunity to discuss various factors that influence social, psychological, and cognitive development in sports leadership and management. Fitness trainers, game officials, tournament managers, recreation attendants, masseurs, and gym instructors are all related professions in this track.


This is more than just dancing and sports. An understanding of human movements through anatomy and physiology is included in Physical Education.

Safety and First Aid are taught as part of the sports track curriculum. This is to ensure that you have the life skills and competencies in safety, injury prevention, and management in a variety of sports and exercise settings to respond quickly and appropriately in an emergency.

The phrase "sports strand" is often used in the context of senior high school education to describe a particular track or strand of study that concentrates on sports and physical education. The sports strand, also known as the Sports Track or Physical Education and Sports Track, is intended for students who are passionate about sports and physical activities. In comparison to the regular curriculum, this track provides a more specialized and in-depth study of sports-related disciplines.


Students who choose the sports strand often take a combination of academic and practical coursework in sports, physical education, fitness, sports science, and related subjects. Some frequent disciplines or areas of study within the sports strand may include:


Sports Coaching: Principles of coaching, sports training methodologies, sports psychology, and athlete development.

Sports Science: Human anatomy and physiology, biomechanics, nutrition, exercise physiology, and sports medicine.

Physical Education: Curriculum development, teaching methodologies, assessment, and the role of physical education in promoting an active and healthy lifestyle.

Sports Management: Principles of sports management, event organization, sports marketing, sports governance, and sports law.

Fitness Training: Principles of fitness training, exercise programming, strength and conditioning, and personal training.

Sports and Recreation Leadership: Leadership skills, teamwork, communication, and decision-making in a sports context.

Students can get a greater understanding of the sports business, develop practical skills, and investigate potential career paths such as coaching, sports administration, fitness training, sports science, or sports management by focusing on the sports strand.


It should be noted that the availability and specific subjects within the sports strand may differ based on the educational system and the offerings of individual schools or institutions. As a result, for more extensive information on the sports strand in senior high school, it is necessary to consult the curriculum guidelines or course catalog of the specific educational institution you are interested in.
