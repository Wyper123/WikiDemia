Arts and Design


The arts and design track will prepare you for a career in the design, performing arts, and creative industries. The goal of this track is to prepare you for a career in the creative field after you graduate. Breaking the stereotype that there are no careers in the arts and design, you'll be surprised at how enjoyable the arts and design world can be.


Part of the curriculum of this track is to expose you to various forms of media such as architecture, interior design, industrial design, graphic design, animation, painting, fashion design, photography, and film to instill the right employment skills so that you can get a job immediately after graduation.


This track is designed for students who want to pursue careers in the performing and creative arts. Discover your abilities and push yourself in the fields of music, theater, visual arts, media arts, and dance. Under this track, you can learn to compose songs, perform on stage, illustrate, sculpt, photograph, or choreograph.


The arts and design strands include different fields of study and vocations relating to artistic expression, creativity, and visual communication. These components are frequently given in educational institutions, particularly high schools and colleges, to provide students with a thorough understanding of art, design concepts, and practical skills. Here are some common arts and design strands:


Performing Arts: This strand encompasses disciplines such as theater, dance, music, and performance. Students develop skills in acting, stagecraft, choreography, musical performance, and music theory. They may also learn about theater history, scriptwriting, and production management.

Media Arts: This strand combines elements of visual arts, design, and technology. Students learn about digital media production, including photography, video production, animation, and digital editing. They may also explore areas like multimedia storytelling, game design, and virtual reality.

Visual Arts: This strand focuses on developing students' skills and knowledge in traditional and contemporary visual arts mediums, such as painting, drawing, sculpture, printmaking, and mixed media. Students may also study art history, art theory, and art criticism.

Graphic Design: This strand emphasizes the principles of design, typography, layout, and digital media. Students learn to create visual communication materials using computer software and gain skills in areas like branding, advertising, web design, and illustration.

Fashion Design: This strand focuses on the creative and technical aspects of fashion design. Students learn about garment construction, pattern-making, fashion illustration, textiles, fashion history, and trends. They also develop skills in fashion styling, merchandising, and marketing.

Industrial Design: Industrial design strand focuses on the design of products and objects for mass production. Students learn about ergonomics, materials, manufacturing processes, 3D modeling, prototyping, and sustainable design practices.

Interior Design: This strand focuses on creating functional and aesthetically pleasing interior spaces. Students learn about space planning, materials and finishes, lighting design, furniture design, and color theory. They may also study architectural drafting and visualization techniques.

Graduates can find many careers in art and design, including animation, fashion, graphic design, and film.
