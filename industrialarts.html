INDUSTRIAL ARTS


Under the Technical-Vocational-Livelihood track, the Industrial Arts strand provides students with the knowledge and skills required for employment. These include technical and manual abilities in the following areas


Construction Painting, Furniture Making, Masonry, and Tile Setting are all examples of carpentry and construction. Electrical and Electronics - Electric Power Distribution Line Construction, Mechatronics Servicing, Assembly and Servicing of Electronic Products Automobiles and Driving - Automobile Service, Driving, Motorcycle/Small Engine Service Domestic Refrigeration and Air Conditioning Servicing

Transmission Line Installation and Maintenance, Electrical Installation and Maintenance are all examples of repair and installation services. Welding techniques include gas metal arc, gas tungsten arc, and shielded metal arc. Plumbing

Finishing a course in the Industrial Arts strand can earn you Certificates of Competency as well as National Certificates in each subject, provided you pass the TESDA assessments. It is critical to obtain these certificates.
