STEM


STEM is a learning and development approach that integrates science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. STEM helps students develop key skills such as problem solving. creativity. and critical thinking. Students are also introduced to complex mathematical and scientific theories and concepts that will serve as a foundation for their college courses.


STEM stands for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics and refers to any of these four disciplines. These four symbols represent the various areas of STEM education.Students in senior high school who pursue the STEM strand are more interested in complex scientific advancements and the future of modern technology.


STEM education is a program designed to prepare primary and secondary students for college, graduate school, and careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM). STEM aims to foster inquiring minds, logical reasoning, and collaboration skills in addition to subject-specific learning.

STEM programs aim to increase the supply of qualified high-tech workers in the United States, boost innovation, improve the country's global competitiveness, and boost economic growth. STEM became popular in the early 2000s as a result of the publication of several reports. The National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine published a report in 2005 titled "Rising Above the Gathering Storm" that emphasized the importance of science and technology jobs in fostering innovation, addressing societal problems, and sustaining US prosperity.

Physics, chemistry, biology, advanced mathematics, computer science, and engineering are common subjects in the STEM strand curriculum. Students are exposed to theoretical concepts as well as hands-on laboratory work and projects to help them build problem-solving and critical thinking abilities.

Students obtain a solid foundation in these topics by focusing on the STEM strand, preparing them for higher education programs or employment in engineering, computer science, biotechnology, medical, research, and other STEM-related professions.
