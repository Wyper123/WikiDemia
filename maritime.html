Pre-baccalaureate Maritime


The Pre-baccalaureate Maritime Specialization is a modified program of the Academic Track's Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) strand. Six STEM strand specialized subjects were retained in this specialization, while three were replaced with maritime-related subjects. The Pre-baccalaureate Maritime Specialization aims to encourage SHS graduates to pursue higher education in the maritime field. The country will be able to produce more officers as a result of this.


This strand focuses on the fundamental, common, and core competencies that every Senior High School (SHS) student should have, and it is intended to improve knowledge, skills, positive attitude, and work values in accordance with workplace standards.


Pre-baccalaureate maritime strands are educational initiatives or academic routes created especially to help students become ready for careers in the maritime sector. These components often offer an extensive education that blends theoretical knowledge with maritime-related practical skills. Typical pre-baccalaureate maritime strands include the following:


Maritime Transportation: This strand focuses on the logistics and management aspects of maritime transportation. Students learn about navigation, ship operations, cargo handling, maritime law, maritime economics, and port management. They may also gain practical experience through simulated ship operations and internships at maritime companies.

Marine Engineering: This strand concentrates on the technical and engineering aspects of ships and maritime structures. Students learn about marine propulsion systems, ship construction, ship machinery, electrical systems, and maintenance. They may also study subjects like thermodynamics, fluid mechanics, and marine safety.

Marine Electro-Technology: This strand emphasizes the electrical and electronic systems used in maritime operations. Students learn about marine electronics, electrical circuits, power distribution systems, automation, and control systems. They may also gain skills in troubleshooting and maintenance of electrical and electronic equipment on ships.

Marine Operations: This strand provides a broader understanding of various maritime operations. Students learn about shipboard operations, navigation, maritime safety, emergency response, maritime regulations, and environmental management. They may also gain practical experience through on-board training programs and internships.

Marine Tourism: This strand focuses on the tourism and hospitality aspects of the maritime industry. Students learn about cruise ship operations, passenger services, tourism marketing, event planning, and customer relations. They may also study topics like destination management, cultural tourism, and sustainability in the tourism sector.

Marine Biology and Aquatic Resources: This strand combines marine science with the study of aquatic ecosystems and resources. Students learn about marine biology, oceanography, fisheries management, marine conservation, and environmental impact assessment. They may also gain practical experience through fieldwork and laboratory research.
